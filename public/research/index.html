Research

My research lies at the intersection of distributed systems, applied cryptography, and data governance.

Research Areas

⚖ Blockchain-Based Data Marketplaces

I design and evaluate decentralized data marketplace architectures, with a strong focus on the energy domain. My work addresses the challenges of privacy, trust, and interoperability in data exchange, integrating technologies such as the Ocean Protocol, IDSA connectors, and GAIA-X dataspaces. A key result is a sub-8 second decentralized marketplace for energy data, demonstrating practical feasibility at scale.

Ocean Protocol IDSA GAIA-X Energy Data Digital Twins

⚙ DAO Governance & Sustainability

I investigate Decentralized Autonomous Organizations (DAOs) through the lens of governance sustainability and longevity. My work includes defining on-chain governance metrics, developing game-theoretic incentive models, and analyzing empirical data from existing DAOs to understand what makes decentralized governance work long-term.

DAOs On-chain Metrics Game Theory Governance Tokenomics

🔒 Privacy-Preserving Cryptographic Techniques

I explore practical applications of advanced cryptographic primitives including Zero-Knowledge (ZK) proofs, Multi-Party Computation (MPC), and Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE). The goal is to bring these techniques from theory to practice in blockchain systems, enabling privacy-preserving computations and verifiable data exchanges.

ZK Proofs MPC FHE SSI Smart Contract Security
