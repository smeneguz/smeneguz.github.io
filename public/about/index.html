About

Researcher, developer, and community builder at the intersection of blockchain and applied cryptography.

Bio

I am a PhD Candidate in Blockchain and Distributed Ledger Technologies within the National PhD Program ISAS (University of Turin – University of Camerino). My research is carried out jointly at the Department of Computer Science, University of Turin and the Links Foundation in Torino, Italy.

My work sits at the intersection of distributed systems, applied cryptography, and software engineering. I focus on designing blockchain-based data marketplaces (with emphasis on the energy domain), investigating DAO governance sustainability through on-chain metrics, and exploring privacy-preserving techniques such as Zero-Knowledge proofs, Multi-Party Computation, and Fully Homomorphic Encryption.

In 2024–2025, I was a visiting researcher at Brunel University London, broadening my perspective on international DLT research.

Beyond research, I am actively involved in community building: I serve as organiser and licensee of TEDxSchio, and I have directed the Startup Grind Padova chapter. I am also an Ocean Protocol Ambassador.
